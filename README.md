<p style="font-family:Arial;font-size:x-large;text-align:justify;word-spacing:0.3em;"><b>PHILLIPINE EAGLE </b><i>(Pithecophaga jefferyi)</i>, also known as the <i>"Monkey-eating Eagle"</i> or <i>"Great Philippine Eagle"</i>, 
is an <b>endangered species</b> of eagle in the family <u>"Accipitridae"</u> which is endemic to the forests in the Philippines. 
It has brown and white-colored plumage, a shaggy crest, and generally measures from 86 to 102 cm in length and weighs from 4.04 to 8.0 kg.
In terms of length and wing surface, it is considered as the largest of the extant eagles in the world.
Among the rarest and most powerful birds, it has been declared as the <b>PHILIPPINE NATIONAL BIRD</b>. 
It is critically endangered, mainly due to massive loss of habitat resulting from deforestation in most of its range. 
Killing a Philippine eagle is punishable under Philippine law by up to 12 years in prison and heavy fines.

<p style="font-family:Impact;font-size:30px">WHERE ARE PHILIPPINE EAGLES LOCATED?</p>
<ul type="square">
<li>The Philippine Eagle, as its name suggests, is found only in the Philippines and can be found on four major islands: Eastern Luzon, Samar, Leyte, and Mindanao.</li>
</ul>

<p style="font-family:Impact;font-size:30px">HOW MANY PHILIPPINE EAGLES ARE LEFT?</p>
<ul type="square">
<li>There are only an estimated 400 nesting pairs of Philippine eagles left in the wild. 
The largest numbers of eagles reside on Mindanao with between 82 and 233 breeding pairs, only six pairs on Samar, two on Leyte, and a few on Luzon.</li>
</ul>

<p style="font-family:Impact;font-size:30px">WHERE DO PHILIPPINE EAGLES INHABIT?</p>
<ul type="square">
<li>Philippine Eagles inhabit in montane forests - mostly in steep and rugged mountains.</li>
</ul>
